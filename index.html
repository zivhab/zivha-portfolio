About

Engineering with intention,
building toward impact

Currently a 2nd-year Computer Science student at the University of Essex, where I ranked in the top 4 in my first year and received the K.F. Bowden Memorial Prize along the way. My focus is on artificial intelligence, data science, and security, three fields that together essentially determine how the next decade of technology gets built.

I'm especially drawn to this intersection, where intelligent systems meet real-world constraints and security isn't an afterthought but a foundation.

Outside of lectures, I've led lessons, projects and communities because I think the people building technology should understand the people it's built for. Most of what I know came from doing it wrong first. I care about the practical side: building things that solve real problems and reshape how we live and work.

Away from the screen, I live in books, speculative fiction or philosophy depending on my mood; in music, classical or K-pop depending on the time of day; and in the kitchen, where baking is my version of debugging life.
